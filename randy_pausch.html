Who is Randy Pausch

  • Carnegie Mellon University professor
  • During the talk, he is battling pancreatic cancer

What was he known for

  • Creator of the Alice software project
  • Human-computer interaction researcher
  • Pioneer in virtual reality
  • Gave the "Last Lecture" titled "Really Achieving Your Childhood Dreams", published a book with the same name "The Last Lecture"

Things that I liked from his talk

  • Calculating what I am worth an hour to make tradeoffs like writing or buying a piece of software.
  • Time >>>>> Money
  • Discard all tasks in to-do list that is not important even if its due soon
  • Having a labeled+sorted file system for easy retrieval (now digital)
  • Always have something to do next immediately to avoid downtime
  • Learn to say no
    • "I will only be your last fallback"
  • Find dead times to do less important tasks
  • Interuptions are very costly
  • Journal what you do with your time to find waste
    • Use your time between class effectively, or even creating a "fake class" yourself.
  • Be efficient to have more time with family
  • Make up fake earlier deadlines to avoid rush and stress
